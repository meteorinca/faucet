What the animation means

In the faucet picture, the handle is the control input. The water stream is the larger flow it allows.

In the transistor picture, the base is the control input. Once the base-emitter junction is biased, current can flow from collector to emitter and the lamp brightens.

The analogy is useful for the idea of control. Real transistors do not contain a little mechanical valve. A BJT works because charge carriers move through semiconductor junctions.
